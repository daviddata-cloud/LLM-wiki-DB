# Sop Doc2 001 Incident Response

CDC Standard Operating Procedure
ID: SOP-DOC2-001
Title: Cybersecurity Incident Response Plan
3.1 Purpose:
This document outlines the procedures for responding to a confirmed cybersecurity incident, including breaches, malware infections, and denial-of-service attacks. The goal is to contain, eradicate, and recover from incidents while minimizing impact.
3.2 Incident Declaration:
The CDC Security Operations Center (SOC) is the sole authority for officially declaring a cybersecurity incident. Upon detection of a potential event, the SOC will perform initial analysis and escalate accordingly.
3.3 Containment Phase:
Upon declaration, the first priority is containment. This may involve isolating affected network segments, disabling compromised user accounts, or blocking traffic from malicious IP addresses. The Incident Response Team Lead will direct all containment activities. Communication during this phase should be handled through secure, out-of-band channels.

CDC Standard Operating Procedure
ID: SOP-DOC3-001
Title: Data Breach Notification and Reporting

1.0 Purpose:
This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the required steps for identifying, reporting, and notifying affected parties in the event of a confirmed data breach involving personally identifiable information (PII) or protected health information (PHI) at the CDC.

2.0 Scope:
This procedure applies to all CDC employees, contractors, and system owners who discover or suspect unauthorized access to sensitive data.

3.0 Breach Identification:
A data breach is defined as any confirmed or suspected unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of sensitive information. Upon discovery, the responsible party must immediately notify the CDC Privacy Officer and the Information Security Officer (ISO) within one (1) hour of detection.
